Grassroots Innovation Taxonomy Repository

NIF-Aligned Local Innovation Classification Framework

Overview

The Grassroots Innovation Taxonomy Repository is a structured classification system designed to identify, document, and organize undocumented grassroots innovations. It aligns with National Innovation Foundation criteria to make community-driven solutions visible, searchable, verifiable, and fundable.

Purpose

  • Classify grassroots innovations using standardized criteria
  • Enable discovery and validation of local solutions
  • Support funding and incubation opportunities
  • Preserve indigenous and community knowledge
  • Bridge informal and formal innovation ecosystems

Taxonomy Domains

Agriculture Healthcare Energy Water & Sanitation Mobility Livelihood Tools Environment Assistive Devices

Use Cases

  • Innovation research and academic study
  • National Innovation Foundation documentation
  • Impact investment discovery
  • Policy and development programs
  • Innovation ecosystem mapping

Mission

To create a global classification infrastructure that enables recognition, documentation, funding, and scaling of grassroots innovations originating from communities worldwide.
